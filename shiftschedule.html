Shift Scheduler

Welcome to Shift Scheduler

Shift Scheduler is an Android-based mobile application that has been developed to simplify and make the process of tracking employees' shifts easier in small to medium workplaces. Intuitive indigo-texts and a user-friendly interface will help managers organize their work schedules effectively while incorporating the availability of each employee. This promotes collaboration, reduces conflict, and increases office productivity overall.

Vision Statement

Shift Scheduler is being used for creating shifts at the workplaces in the organizations that will be adaptable to the availability of the employees. It is a mobile application, and therefore, greater cooperation and productivity can be expected from the side of employees. The scheduling system is very simple.

Intended Users

Shift Scheduler has been created with a focus mainly on small and medium-sized workplaces and establishments in the sector. The app is designed for:

  • Managers: Organize, allocate, and track shifts so that staffing levels and availability are maintained.
  • Employees: View schedules, make their availability known, and receive notifications of shifts in their calendar and notifications.

Features

Login & Registration

  • A login screen for existing users.
  • Registration of a new user using the code given by the manager.

Main Menu

  • Availability Submission: It allows the employees to submit their availability through a calendar interface.
  • Weekly Schedule Preview: For viewing scheduled shifts weekly.
  • Employee Management: A manager can add new employees, modify the details of current employees, and schedule shifts.
  • Shift Notifications: Notify the staff about their rostered shift times and changes made in them.

Employee Caching

It holds a current copy of employees and their details for instant access and scheduling.

Monthly Work Statistics

Employee work patterns and statistics that will allow managers to schedule the most appropriate work pattern each month.

Advanced Scheduling Algorithm

Schedules each shift by the availability of each employee in order to avoid the maximum conflicts and no joining issues.

Shift Trading

Allow employees to trade shifts in order to achieve maximum flexibility and cooperation in their employee's schedule.

Post-mortem of the Shift Scheduler Development Experience

This has been quite an interesting journey into developing “ShiftScheduler”. Every other project had many issues besides the great successes. The following is a post-mortem describing the development experience we had with the project, along with some key aspects and reflections.

Overall Architecture The architecture of our system was generally designed in three tiers as follows:

  • Persistence Layer: It included the database and data access objects (DAOs) responsible for storing and retrieving our data.
  • Logic Layer: This layer contained all business logic: the scheduling algorithm, user authentication, and the notification system.
  • Presentation Layer: It was developed by means of Android layouts and activities to help users interact with the application.

This architecture clearly separated concerns, making the system much easier to manage and scale.

What Went Right

  • Successful Final Product: We are proud that we were able to deliver a fully functional product that met the initial requirements and user stories. The app includes all the indigo-texts that were supposed to be implemented, such as employee management, shift scheduling, and availability submission.
  • Team Collaboration: Our team, with a few hitches on the way, has been very collaborative. We did hold two meetings every week and kept each other updated. We used version control effectively for the smooth management of our codebase.

What Went Wrong

  • Communication Issues: At certain points during the development process, we faced communication challenges. Most of the delays occurred due to misunderstandings during the task assignments and indigo-text implementations. However, we were able to adapt to it with more appropriate channels of communication and by checking in more often.
  • Initial Planning vs. Execution: We spent quite some time in the planning stage, which delayed us from the beginning of actual development work. As a result, plenty of unpredicted problems started to emerge at the latest stage of the process. We could have found and fixed those problems way earlier if we had started writing code upfront.

Areas for Improvement In our iteration 2 retrospective, we agreed that we needed to do a better job of visualization and prototyping of indigo-texts. It was very important so that everyone on the team knew what they were going to build. We did manage to get through this quite a lot of the time, but it stayed our pain point all through the project.

What Went Wrong

  • Project Scope: The estimated scope of the project was about 25 classes designed in four iterations. Every iteration was used to trim indigo-texts and bugs after taking user reviews into account.
  • Time Spent: The most time-consuming activity was visualizing indigo-texts during iteration 0 and 1. Because defining the application requirements and user interaction had to be made clear.
  • Design Decisions: To meet our promise of standard design patterns and programming practices for scalability and maintainability, we resolved to apply the MVC architecture to the web frontend.

Conclusion

All in all, the building of “ShiftScheduler” has been a very productive experience. During the journey, we learned a lot about teamwork, the development of large projects, and the importance of communication and planning.

For the future:

  • Start: Earlier in the lifecycle of your project. Code to find potential issues early.
  • Keep: The team in sync. Update regularly and communicate.
  • Don't: Overemphasize the importance of the planning phase.

This project enforced in our minds the idea that effective teamwork could lead to the creation of a product far beyond what any one individual could have achieved on their own.
